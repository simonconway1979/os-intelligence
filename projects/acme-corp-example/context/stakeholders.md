# Stakeholder Dynamics — Acme Corp

Synthesised across LinkedIn bios, predecessor handoff, all-hands transcript, board update, and the 360 review of Priya Nair. These are reads to calibrate against direct observation in Month 1, not facts.

## The Most Important Reads

### Marcus Webb — CPO (Simon's manager)
- Hired Simon. Forwarded the predecessor handoff memo before Simon's start date — good faith.
- Conflict-averse with the exec team. **More candid in 1:1s than in exec product meetings**. Positions taken privately may not be defended publicly.
- Slow to respond verbally — better to default to written/async requests.
- Held the Head of Product seat as interim from Sam's departure (Feb 2026) until Simon's arrival.
- Public framing: "next 18 months will define the category."
- Made the call to decline Priya Nair's Staff promotion (cited insufficient product-level impact). Kasia Nowak contested the reasoning as circular in the written record. Tension Simon will inherit.
- Background: Helios Software Group (£200M+ ARR enterprise product, led AI integration 2021); INSEAD MBA; Oxford PPE.

### Elena Sousa — CRO
- Joined July 2024, recruited by Johnny ahead of Series C close. Co-led "AI-First" positioning with Johnny.
- **Will close deals on commitments product hasn't made.** Will run the roadmap commercially if allowed to.
- Sam Reilly: managing this relationship — professionally, without conflict — is "the central political skill of this role." Sam was self-assessed as not good at holding the line.
- Recommended mitigation (from Sam): written delivery constraints to Elena, repeated quarterly.
- Anonymised colleague quote: "She makes commitments that engineering then has to deliver. That's the job. It's also the friction."
- History: Two clean exits at Verispan (acquired by Workday) and Skyline Learning (left over founder pivot). Demonstrated willingness to exit when direction diverges.

### Kasia Nowak — Head of Engineering, Warsaw
- **Most important relationship for the AI roadmap.** Owns the prototype that Acme's AI-First commitment hangs on.
- **Does not currently trust London product** to make commitments she can deliver. Trust must be rebuilt — accumulated through two prior AI failures.
- Sam's explicit recommendation: visit Warsaw in Month 1 (not Month 3) — flagged as a gesture she will value.
- Background: Spotify (search/recommendations), Cargolytics (route optimisation Principal Eng); MSc CS (ML), University of Warsaw.
- Builds and protects culture — hired most of her team. Direct, technically deep, prioritises honest over comfortable.
- Recruited Priya Nair from market via existing Spotify-overlap relationship.
- Gave PyData Warsaw 2025 talk with Priya: "Why Your Bias Audit Is Not Enough." Strong views on AI safety/fairness.
- Publicly opposed the calibration committee's decision to deny Priya promotion in the written record.

### Tom Okafor — Head of Engineering, London
- 4 years at Acme. Has watched both prior AI failures. Cautious for legitimate reasons.
- Sam's framing: **"Not a blocker — a quality filter. Treat as partner; going around him will mean not shipping."**
- Operating principle: "Engineering reality is the gravity of every commitment."
- Prior employer (Aspora) over-promised on AI personalisation, shipped a feature that didn't work, got acquired below post-money — formative scepticism.
- Vinod Mehta (Sales): "the best Head of Engineering I have worked with in 15 years" — strong Sales-Engineering alignment may be stronger than current Product-Engineering alignment.
- Stress-tested the August demo timeline at Q1 all-hands (asked Johnny what happens if security review surfaces issue mid-May; got Johnny on record: customer launch over conference demo).

### James Whitfield — Head of Customer Success
- **Has the most accurate read on customer reality of anyone at the company.** Has been raising concerns that have not been listened to internally (reporting parity from late 2024; candidate data governance from mid-2025).
- Sam's recommendation: structured weekly with James as a CS canary.
- "I would rather be wrong out loud than right in private." Will challenge in public settings.
- Prior breach experience at Aurelia Financial (2021) shaped a strong professional habit of being "right out loud."
- Surfaced that 2 enterprise customers using ChatGPT directly with candidate data, outside any Acme governance — at Q1 all-hands.
- Authored internal post-loss notes on Northwind Bank churn.

### Priya Nair — Data Scientist, Warsaw (Applied Research, reports to Kasia)
- **Originating author of the technical work underlying Acme's flagship AI commitment.**
- Brought candidate-matching prototype to Sam twice (May 2025, Dec 2025); both times deprioritised under Marcus's direction.
- That prototype is now central to AI-First.
- H2 2025 review: Solid Performer (3/5). Promotion to Staff declined by Marcus Webb's calibration committee. Signed her review with a formal note of disagreement.
- Kasia formally contested the committee reasoning as circular — work was deprioritised by Product, then absence of product adoption used to deny promotion.
- Background: DeepMind (alignment research), Spotify (recommendations); MSc ML IIScI; BTech CS IIT Madras.
- Co-organiser, ML Fairness Reading Group Warsaw. Co-author with Mihaela Văduva (ETH Zurich) on FAccT papers (2023, 2024) on counterfactual fairness in ranking + bias propagation in multi-tenant embedding spaces.
- 360 feedback from 8 respondents: "strongest applied researcher in the company," "most undervalued person in the engineering org" — common views, not outliers.
- **High retention risk.** Likely to engage Simon early with significant context and unresolved expectations. Sam: explicit advice to acknowledge her contribution publicly to the engineering team.
- Loyalty anchor: Kasia. Joined Acme because of Kasia's pitch.

### Johnny Rotten — CEO
- Made the public AI-First commitment — partly defensive (acknowledged), partly ambitious.
- Stated priority if timelines diverge: customer launch over conference demo.
- Open-door: third-floor kitchen, 09:00–09:20 most mornings. Email johnny@acme-corp.com (calendar via Aisha).
- Explicitly committed not to trade platform reliability for AI speed.
- Will reference Simon's first six weeks in the 7 June 2026 investor update — Simon has external visibility from day one.
- Co-founder.

### Wei Chen — VP Engineering

- Candid about engineering reality. Wrote unusually honest tech stack overview for new starters.
- Co-owns quarterly product-engineering joint review with Marcus.
