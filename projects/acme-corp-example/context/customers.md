# Customers — Acme Corp

## Base

- 220+ enterprise customers
- 4M+ applications processed annually
- Geos: UK, Europe, North America (NA expansion is the next wave)

**Named customers (active):** Avlon Insurance, Mercer-Lawson Consulting

**Recent churn:** Northwind Bank (£312k ARR, Q1 2026), Hartfield Retail Group (Q4 2025) — both traced to legacy reporting decommissioning

## Buyer Dynamics

- Enterprise customers (particularly FS) are evaluating Acme alongside AI-native competitors
- Buyers value Acme for reliability; want AI features from alternatives
- 3 of top 30 accounts have requested formal AI briefings (per Q4 2025 investor update)
- Six of top 30 accounts named a specific competitor by name in 2025 sales cycles
- Two renewals had AI feature dates as conditions
- At least 2 enterprise customers using ChatGPT directly with candidate data, outside any Acme governance (raised by James Whitfield at Q1 all-hands; ownership of resolution unclear)

## Anna Lindgren's Q1 All-Hands Question (the central tension)

> "Customers choose us for reliability, AI-native competitors for AI features. How do we maintain both stories simultaneously?"

Johnny called it "the central question of the year."

## Northwind Bank Churn (deep context)

- **ARR lost:** £312,000
- **Customer since:** 2022-04
- **Non-renewal effective:** 2026-03-25
- **Account owner (closed):** Vinod Mehta. CS owner: Maya Hartwell.
- **Decision-maker on non-renewal:** Sandra Eisenberg (TA Director, Northwind Bank)

**Proximal cause:** 6 weeks of broken reporting on 3 business-critical reports after legacy suite was deprecated without parity (Jan 2026 escalation).

**Underlying cause:** AI capability gap. Northwind had been evaluating an AI-native competitor for 4 months. That competitor's AI screening was already live at 2 peer banks. Acme's AI roadmap promised August 2026; competitor was already in production. Northwind's CHRO: "what is our AI roadmap?" — Acme had no credible near-term answer.

**Sandra Eisenberg's exit:** Personally praised Maya Hartwell ("the relationship has been a strong one, and the decision is not a personal one"). Agreed to a structured exit interview. James Whitfield owns follow-through.

**James Whitfield's internal lesson:** "Without the reporting failure, Northwind was prepared to give us another year on AI maturity grounds — the reporting failure forced a decision the AI gap had already made likely."

## Implication for Simon

- Enterprise accounts with complex reporting needs are at churn risk. Audit how many other accounts on the new reporting product have unresolved parity gaps.
- "AI-First by 2026" must be stress-tested against what August 2026 actually delivers vs what competitors have in production today.
- 14 customers remain on legacy reporting suite. Migrations frozen pending parity. James Whitfield's CS team managing direct lines.
- CS communication is strong at leadership level but support-tier response times for enterprise accounts are flagged as a risk (6 weeks open before Northwind escalated).
