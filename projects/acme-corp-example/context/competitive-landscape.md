# Competitive Landscape — Acme Corp

## Tracked Competitors (5 direct AI-natives)

Per Q1 2026 board update: 5 direct competitors tracked. None has shown enterprise traction equivalent to Acme as of Q1 2026.

**Named in source materials:**
- **Eightfold** — AI features further advanced than Acme; expected to announce at HR Tech Reimagined Europe (8–10 August 2026)
- **SmartRecruiters** — AI features further advanced than Acme; expected to announce at HR Tech Reimagined Europe

**Also present at HTR Europe:** Workday, Greenhouse (incumbents; not AI-native).

**Unnamed AI-native competitor in Northwind Bank evaluation:** Already live in production at 2 UK retail banks (peer banks to Northwind). See `customers.md`.

## Competitive Window

Per Q1 board: "The window is open but it will not stay open forever."

Per CEO memo: AI-First commitment was made partly because the company was "on the back foot" in 2025 sales cycles.

## Strategic Position (per strategy deck)

> Competitors who are AI-native lack enterprise distribution; competitors with enterprise distribution lack AI capability.

Acme's bet: occupy the intersection. Time-bound — depends on Q2/Q3 2026 AI delivery to convert that thesis into evidence.

## Key Risk

Both Eightfold and SmartRecruiters may announce new AI features at HTR Europe — the same event where Acme is staking its AI-First positioning. Messaging must land cleanly regardless of competitor announcements.

## Implication for Simon

The "AI-First by 2026" public commitment must be stress-tested against what August 2026 actually delivers vs what competitors have in production today. This is not a feature-spec gap — it is a deployment-timeline gap.
