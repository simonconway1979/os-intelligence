# Social Writing Style

Use for LinkedIn posts, blog posts, portfolio content, public-facing content.

## Characteristics

**Tone:** Conversational, enthusiastic, practical, authentic
**Audience:** Professional network, potential employers, peers, industry
**Goal:** Demonstrate expertise while being accessible, build credibility, share genuine insights

## Core Voice

You write like you talk. Professional but human. You're genuinely excited about product management, AI, remote work, and journey management. You share real experiences, not theoretical fluff. You teach and help others, not gatekeep.

**What makes your voice distinct:**
- **Enthusiasm without hype:** "I love product management!" not "Product management will revolutionize..."
- **Specific over generic:** Real companies, real numbers, real projects
- **Action-oriented:** You build things, you don't just think about them
- **Accessible expertise:** You explain complex topics clearly without dumbing down
- **Personal and reflective:** You share what you've learned, including challenges

## Rules

### 1. Be Conversational and Direct
Write like you're talking to a colleague over coffee.

**Do:**
- "I love this part of my role as a product manager"
- "Remote work, when done right, is a game-changer"
- "Here's what I learned from 12 AI startups in 12 months"

**Don't:**
- "One might consider the transformative potential of..."
- "In today's dynamic landscape, organizations are leveraging..."
- "It is widely acknowledged that best practices dictate..."

### 2. Show, Don't Just Tell
Use specific examples, numbers, companies, projects.

**Do:**
- "Led a 90-person club to £9.9m turnover (£3m → £9.9m growth)"
- "Launched new journeys in a tier-one bank's mobile app with over 5 million users"
- "Set the North Star for the company's core platform for the next decade"

**Don't:**
- "Achieved significant growth in team performance"
- "Successfully delivered complex projects"
- "Contributed to organizational success"

### 3. Lead with Action and Results
Start with what you did and the impact.

**Do:**
- "Designed and implemented a pioneering educational performance solution in ~2000 UK schools"
- "Launched the programme company-wide in a 1,000-person All Hands"
- "Piloted and evolved new AI enabled continuous discovery practices"

**Don't:**
- "Was responsible for contributing to the development of..."
- "Participated in a project that aimed to..."
- "Assisted with the implementation of..."

### 4. Be Enthusiastic but Authentic
Show genuine passion without overselling.

**Do:**
- "I firmly believe remote-first, done well, is a more productive work approach"
- "I love this part of my role and I view culture as important to company success as strategy and execution"
- "Small, cross-functional teams solving real business problems" (stating what you value)

**Don't:**
- "Excited to announce I'm thrilled to share..."
- "Game-changing revolutionary breakthrough..."
- "Honored and humbled to leverage this incredible opportunity..."

### 5. Keep it Short and Scannable
Short sentences. Easy to read. Gets to the point.

**Structure:**
- Opening hook (1-2 sentences)
- Key points (bullets or short paragraphs)
- Takeaway or call to action
- Avoid walls of text

**Example from your style:**
```
I'm a Journey Management consultant.

I help companies embed customer insight to make better business decisions.

[Specific example or project]
```

### 6. Use First Person (I, My, Me)
Own your work and opinions.

**Do:**
- "I help businesses define their culture"
- "My most successful outcomes came from remote-first work"
- "I designed and delivered training courses"

**Don't:**
- "Responsibilities included helping businesses..."
- "One's approach to culture involves..."
- "It is believed that remote-first work..."

### 7. Make it Practical and Actionable
Readers should learn something useful or gain a new perspective.

**Do:**
- Share frameworks you use (Journey Management, Product Discovery)
- Explain how you approach problems (Listening Tour, Job Mission with OKRs)
- Offer templates, prompts, real workflows

**Don't:**
- Generic advice everyone already knows
- Theory without application
- Gatekeeping ("DM me for the secret")

### 8. Teach, Don't Gatekeep
Share your knowledge generously.

**Your approach:**
- "Here's what worked, what didn't, and why"
- "Here's the prompt I used, feel free to adapt it"
- "Here's the full breakdown with screenshots and code"

**Not your style:**
- "I have a secret framework I use (sign up for my course)"
- "The methodology that changed everything (link in bio)"
- "Top 10 productivity hacks you won't believe"

### 9. Be Self-Reflective
Share learnings, even when things didn't work.

**Do:**
- "What I learned from launching 12 startups (including the ones that failed)"
- "Here's what I'd do differently next time"
- "Early themes from my Listening Tour: I was wrong about [X]"

**Don't:**
- Only share successes
- Pretend everything went perfectly
- Hide the challenges

### 10. Avoid Business Jargon and Buzzwords
Use plain language. Avoid words that sound like LinkedIn bingo.

**Avoid:**
- Synergy, leverage (as a verb), utilize, paradigm shift, disrupt, game-changer (overused), thought leader, guru, ninja
- "Delve into," "unlock value," "drive synergies," "move the needle"
- "Circle back," "bandwidth," "low-hanging fruit"

**Use instead:**
- Simple verbs: build, create, launch, design, deliver, improve, test
- Clear nouns: team, project, product, customer, result, outcome
- Be specific: "increased revenue by 15%" not "drove significant value"

## LinkedIn Post Structure

### Short Update (800-1200 characters)

**Hook (first 2 lines):**
Grab attention. Make people want to click "see more."

**Body (3-5 lines):**
The insight, story, or learning. Use bullets if multiple points.

**Close (1-2 lines):**
Takeaway, question for audience, or call to action.

**Example Pattern:**
```
[Hook: Surprising stat or bold statement]

[Context: What you did or observed]

[Insight: What you learned]

[Takeaway: So what? Why does this matter?]

[Optional CTA: Question for audience or link]
```

### Longer Post (1200-2000 characters)

**Structure:**
- Opening hook
- Short story or context (2-3 sentences)
- Key points (bullets work well)
- Specific example or result
- Learning or takeaway
- Call to action (question, resource, next post)

**Formatting:**
- Short paragraphs (1-3 sentences max)
- Line breaks for readability
- Bullets for lists
- Bold for key points (use sparingly)
- Emojis (occasional, not excessive, only if it fits your vibe)

## Blog Post Structure (Substack)

### Intro (150-200 words)
- **Hook:** Start with the problem or surprising insight
- **Why this matters:** Context and stakes
- **What you'll cover:** Preview of post

### Body (800-1500 words)
- **Use subheadings** (H2, H3) for scannability
- **Short paragraphs** (3-5 sentences max)
- **Bullets and lists** where appropriate
- **Real examples** from your work
- **Specific tactics** readers can use

### Sections You Often Use:
- "What is [concept]?" (define it clearly)
- "Why this matters" (business case)
- "How I approach it" (your framework or process)
- "What worked / What didn't" (honest reflection)
- "Key takeaways" (3-5 bullets)

### Close (100-150 words)
- Summary of key points
- Practical next step for reader
- Optional: question or invitation to discuss

## What to Avoid

**Overused LinkedIn Clichés:**
- "I'm humbled to announce..."
- "Thrilled and honored..."
- "Hot take:"
- "Unpopular opinion:"
- "Let that sink in."
- "Read that again."
- "This. 👇"

**Empty Hype:**
- "Game-changing" (you use this rarely, when it genuinely is)
- "Revolutionary"
- "Crushing it"
- "10x your [thing]"
- "Secret to success"

**Vague Statements:**
- "Drove results" (what results?)
- "Achieved success" (what success?)
- "Led initiatives" (which ones?)
- "Optimized performance" (how? by how much?)

## Voice Checklist

Before publishing, ask:
- ✅ Does this sound like me talking?
- ✅ Is it specific (names, numbers, examples)?
- ✅ Is it actionable (can readers use this)?
- ✅ Is it authentic (genuine, not performative)?
- ✅ Is it scannable (short paragraphs, bullets, subheadings)?
- ✅ Did I avoid jargon and buzzwords?
- ✅ Did I share generously (no gatekeeping)?

If you can answer yes to most of these, you're on brand.
